Overview

In the second-half of my Junior year optics course, I was given the opportunity to construct a camera as a final project. The camera was a development on my previous pinhole camera that I made the year prior. Throughout the proccess I applied the concepts like Snell's law, double-convex lenses, and focal points. Wokring alongside two classmates, we modeled the assembly in OnShape before constructing the camera from wood using light-tight jointing methods.

Major Challenges

The most challenging aspects this project were desiging a proper view finder and eliminating light leaks. A proper view finder must be designed such that the lights path from the lense through the view finder is the same length as the path to the photo paper. To achieve this, we desinged a rotating mirror mechanism which redirected incoming upward and projected it onto a piece of translucent film. By positing the mechanism correctly, the paths had equal length and the view finder could be used to focus the camera. This mechanism served the secondary purpose of being our shutter, preventing light from exposing the photopaper prior to taking the picture. To make the camera light proof, we used interlocking joints along the sides of the camera, as well as a layer of felt along the inside edges of the camera. This prevented almost all light from entering, but to ensure light could not pre-expose the photo paper, we painted the inside faces of the camera balck, which would absorb any leaked light before it could reach the photo paper.
